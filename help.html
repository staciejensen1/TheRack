Getting Started

Logging In

  1. Open the app at app.therackapp.io
  2. Enter the email address you used when purchasing
  3. Enter your Access Code (sent to you via email after purchase)
  4. Click Log In

Your credentials are saved automatically, so you will be logged in on future visits.

First-Time Setup

After logging in for the first time:

  1. Click Settings in the sidebar
  2. Enter your business information (Business Name, Owner Name, Contact Info)
  3. Set your default species (e.g., "Ball Python")
  4. Click Save Settings

Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard is your home base. It shows everything important at a glance.

Alerts Section

Alerts appear at the top when action is needed. They are color-coded by urgency (darker = more urgent):

Click any alert card to jump directly to the filtered view.

Stats Sections

  • Breeders - Total count, males, females, and holdbacks
  • Incubator - Incubating clutches, hatched this year, average days to hatch, average fertile eggs
  • Hatchlings - Listed, unlisted, male/female counts
  • Sales (This Year) - Sold count, revenue, on hold, unshipped
  • Sales (Last Year) - Previous year comparison

Managing Your Collection

The Collection tab shows all your breeding animals.

Adding a Breeder

  1. Click + Breeder in the sidebar or quick add bar
  2. Fill in the required fields:
    • Animal Name - Your name for this animal
    • Sex - Female, Male, or Unknown
    • Date of Birth - Required for maturity calculation
    • Species - Defaults to your setting
    • Genetic Summary - List genetics (e.g., "Pastel Het Clown")
    • Status - Defaults to "Breeder"
  3. Click Save

A unique ID is automatically generated (e.g., B-2026-0001).

Filtering and Searching

Use the filter bar above the table to narrow your view:

  • Search - Type a name or ID
  • Sex - Filter by Female, Male, or Unknown
  • Status - Filter by any status
  • Species - Filter by species
  • Gene Tags - Click any gene tag in the table to filter by that gene

Click Clear Filters to reset.

Maturity Calculation

The app automatically calculates maturity based on Date of Birth:

  • HTCH - Hatchling (under 6 months)
  • SUB - Sub-adult (6-18 months)
  • ADULT - Adult (over 18 months)

Tracking Clutches

The Clutches tab tracks all your breeding clutches from lay to hatch.

Adding a Clutch

  1. Click + Clutch in the sidebar
  2. Fill in the fields:
    • Sire - Select from your male breeders
    • Dam - Select from your female breeders
    • Lay Date - When eggs were laid
    • Number of Eggs - Total eggs
    • Number Fertile - Fertile eggs
    • Number Slugs - Infertile eggs
  3. Click Save

A clutch ID is automatically generated (e.g., CL-2026-0001). The Estimated Hatch Date is automatically calculated (Lay Date + 59 days).

Hatching a Clutch

When your clutch hatches:

  1. Find the clutch in the table
  2. Click the Hatch button
  3. Enter the Hatch Date
  4. Enter the Number of Hatchlings
  5. Choose an option:
    • Mark Hatched Only - Just updates the clutch status
    • Hatch and Create Hatchlings - Creates individual hatchling records

If you choose to create hatchlings, a form appears for each one where you can set Sex, Name, Status, and Genetic Summary. The Sire and Dam are inherited from the clutch.

Managing Hatchlings

The Hatchlings tab shows all animals with Listed, Unlisted, or For Sale status.

Adding a Hatchling Manually

  1. Click + Hatchling in the sidebar
  2. Select the Clutch ID (required) - this links to the clutch and auto-fills Sire/Dam
  3. Enter Hatch Date
  4. Set Sex, Status, and Genetic Summary
  5. Optional: Add Hatch Weight
  6. Click Save

Quick Actions

Each hatchling row has action buttons:

  • Edit - Modify any field
  • Sell - Open the sale form
  • + Holdback - Change status to Holdback
  • Delete - Move to Archive

Recording Pairings

The Pairings tab shows all breeding activity (Paired and Lock records).

Recording a Pairing

  1. Click + Activity in the sidebar
  2. Select the Animal (the female being paired)
  3. Set Activity to "Paired" or "Lock"
  4. In the Paired With field, select the male
  5. Enter the Date
  6. Click Save

Pairings View

The Pairings table shows:

  • Date - When the pairing occurred
  • Sire - The male
  • Dam - The female
  • Activity - Paired or Lock

Use the search bar to find specific animals.

Sales Management

The Sales tab shows all On Hold and Sold animals.

Recording a Sale

  1. Find the animal in the Hatchlings tab
  2. Click the Sell button
  3. Fill in the sale form:
    • Sold Date - Date of sale
    • Sold Price - Sale price
    • Amount Paid - How much the buyer has paid
    • Shipping Fee - Shipping cost
    • Buyer Name - Customer name
    • Buyer Email - Customer email
    • Sale Source - MorphMarket, Instagram, Facebook, etc.
    • Payment Status - Deposit, Paid, or Refunded
    • Ship Date - When it will ship
  4. Click Save Sale

Tracking Partial Payments

If a buyer pays a deposit:

  1. Set Payment Status to "Deposit"
  2. Enter the deposit amount in Amount Paid
  3. When fully paid, edit the record and update Amount Paid to the full amount

The Balance Due alert appears when Amount Paid is less than Sold Price.

Adding an Activity

  1. Click + Activity in the sidebar
  2. Select the Animal from the dropdown
  3. Choose the Activity type
  4. Enter a Value if needed (weight, notes, etc.)
  5. For Paired/Lock activities, select Paired With
  6. Set the Date
  7. Click Save

Archive and Data Recovery

When you delete an animal or clutch, it moves to the Archive rather than being permanently deleted.

Viewing the Archive

  1. Click Archive in the navigation
  2. See all archived animals and clutches
  3. View the date each item was archived

Restoring from Archive

  1. Find the item in the Archive
  2. Click Restore
  3. The item returns to its original tab

Importing from MorphMarket

Import your existing MorphMarket inventory in seconds.

Exporting from MorphMarket

  1. Log in to MorphMarket
  2. Go to My Store then Inventory
  3. Click Export to download a CSV file

Importing to The Rack

  1. Click Import from MorphMarket in the sidebar
  2. Click Choose File and select your CSV
  3. Review the preview:
    • New - Animals not in your collection
    • Update - Animals that already exist (matched by Animal ID)
  4. Click Import All

QR Code System

Every animal gets a unique QR code for quick access.

Printing QR Codes

  1. Click Print QR Codes in the sidebar
  2. Choose which animals to print:
    • All Animals
    • Breeders Only
    • Hatchlings Only
  3. Choose label size: Small (1"), Medium (1.5"), or Large (2")
  4. Click Print

Using QR Codes

  1. Print and attach labels to your tubs or racks
  2. Scan a QR code with your phone camera
  3. The app opens directly to that animal
  4. Log activity immediately (feeding, shed, weight, etc.)

This is the fastest way to record daily husbandry.

Bin Tags and Invoices

Printing Bin Tags

  1. Find the animal in the Collection or Hatchlings tab
  2. Click Edit to open the record
  3. Click Print Bin Tag
  4. A printable tag opens with: Animal ID, Name, Genetics, DOB, Sex, and QR Code

Generating Invoices

  1. Find a sold animal in the Sales tab
  2. Click Edit to open the record
  3. Click Generate Invoice
  4. A printable invoice opens with your business info, buyer info, animal details, and pricing breakdown

Tips and Best Practices

Daily Workflow

  1. Check the Dashboard for alerts
  2. Address overdue items first
  3. Log feeding and activities via QR codes
  4. Update statuses as things change

Naming Conventions

Use consistent naming to make searching easier:

  • Breeders: Descriptive names (e.g., "Pastel Clown Female 1")
  • Hatchlings: Wait until they develop patterns or keep generic until sold

Genetic Summary Format

Be consistent with how you list genetics:

  • List visual genes first
  • Use "Het" for heterozygous genes
  • Use "Poss Het" for possible hets
  • Example: "Pastel Banana Het Clown Poss Het Pied"

Regular Syncing

Click Sync regularly to ensure your data is current, especially if you use multiple devices.

Backup Your Data

Export your collection and clutches periodically as a backup. Your data lives in Google Sheets, which is automatically backed up, but local copies are good practice.

Use the Archive

Do not worry about deleting animals. Everything goes to Archive first, so you can always restore if needed.
